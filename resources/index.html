Resources

This page is dedicated to resources we strongly recommend to anyone looking to grow and optimize their skill set.

Books

  • The Leasing Process: Landlord and Tenant Perspectives

    by Richard F. Muhlebach, Alan A. Alexander

    Description: This book provides a comprehensive review of the lease negotiation process, elucidating the unique perspectives of both the landlord and the tenant. As veteran real estate management professionals with over 70 years combined experience in the field, the authors share the key components that go into each party's role in the leasing process, from the initial stages through execution. Landlords, asset managers, brokers, and tenants alike will gain tips for handling the toughest of negotiations in order to achieve a win-win outcome.

  • How to Win Friends and Influence People

    by Dale Carnegie

    Description: This grandfather of all people-skills books was first published in 1937. It was an overnight hit, eventually selling 15 million copies. How to Win Friends and Influence People is just as useful today as it was when it was first published, because Dale Carnegie had an understanding of human nature that will never be outdated.

  • The War of Art: Break Through the Blocks and Win Your Inner Creative Battles

    by Steven Pressfield

    Description: Drawing on his many years' experience as a writer, Pressfield (The Legend of Bagger Vance) presents his first nonfiction work, which aims to inspire other writers, artists, musicians, or anyone else attempting to channel his or her creative energies. While certainly of great value to frustrated writers struggling with writer's block, Pressfield's highly personal philosophy, soundly rooted in his own significant life challenges, has merit for anyone frustrated in fulfilling his or her life purpose.

  • Getting to Yes: Negotiating Agreement Without Giving In

    by Roger Fisher

    Description: Since its original publication nearly thirty years ago, Getting to Yes has helped millions of people learn a better way to negotiate. One of the primary business texts of the modern era, it is based on the work of the Harvard Negotiation Project, a group that deals with all levels of negotiation and conflict resolution. Getting to Yes offers a proven, step-by-step strategy for coming to mutually acceptable agreements in every sort of conflict.

  • The Book of Five Rings

    by Miyamoto Musashi

    Description: When the undefeated samurai Miyamoto Musashi retreated to a cave in 1643 and wrote The Book of Five Rings, a manifesto on swordsmanship, strategy, and winning for his students and generations of samurai to come, he created one of the most perceptive and incisive texts on strategic thinking ever to come from Asia. Musashi gives timeless advice on defeating an adversary, throwing an opponent off-guard, creating confusion, and other techniques for overpowering an assailant that will resonate with both martial artists and everyone else interested in skillfully dealing with conflict. For Musashi, the way of the martial arts was a mastery of the mind rather than simply technical prowess.

Tools

  • BOMA Standards

    by Building Owners and Managers Association International

    Description: The Building Owners and Management Association International sets the standard for measuring buildings. They are the best resource for understanding the methods of measurement for different property types.

  • DocuSign

    by DocuSign

    Description: DocuSign provides a means for obtaining digital signature that can help streamline your transaction processes.
