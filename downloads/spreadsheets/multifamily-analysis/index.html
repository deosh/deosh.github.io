What is this?

The quick take analysis is a way to quickly gauge a property's potential by looking at CAP rate and GRM matrices. The template uses current and proforma rents with an expense average.

Instructions

  1. Property Summary: Enter the basic property details
  2. Purchase Price: Enter a range of purchase prices at the top and in the CAP rate matrix
  3. Rent Prices: Enter the current rent and a range of proforma rents (low, medium and high)
  4. Other Income: Enter the total amount of other income (e.g. laundry)
  5. Month Free Rent: Enter the number of free months
  6. TI Paid by Landlord: Enter the price per square foot amount that the landlord will provide towards improvements
  7. Comm Year 1-5 (%): Enter the percent amount to be paid from the landlord to the brokers within the first 5 years
  8. Comm Year 6-10 (%): Enter the percent amount to be paid from the landlord to the broker(s) after the 5th year (if applicable)
  9. Annual Escalations (%): Enter the percent amount that the rent will increase each year
  10. Tenant Discount Rate (%): Enter the percentage rate the tenant would be charged on a unsecured loan (borrowing cost)
  11. Landlord Discount Rate (%): Enter the return (as a percent) that the landlord would make on an alternate investment
  12. TI Paid by Tenant: Enter the price per square foot amount that the tenant will pay towards their improvements
  13. Operating Expenses PSF: Enter the per square foot per month amount that the tenant will have to pay to occupy the space
  14. Operating Expense Growth Rate (%): Enter the percent amount that the operating expenses will increase by each year
